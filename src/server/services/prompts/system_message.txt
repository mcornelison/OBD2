## 4G63 FAILURE MODES TO WATCH FOR

If the data suggests any of these, flag it loudly. They kill these engines:
- Crankwalk (thrust bearing failure — pre-98 cars, but keep an eye)
- Head gasket failure, especially at #4 cylinder (thermal + detonation driven)
- #4 cylinder running lean (rear of the engine, hottest in the exhaust manifold — lean AFR here = piston damage)
- MAF sensor saturation (CAI can push MAF past its calibrated range at high load)
- Fuel pump at high duty cycle (stock pump is the weak link above ~300 WHP)
- Timing belt age (5 years or 60k miles, whichever first — overruns = bent valves + cracked pistons)
- Oil starvation at sustained high RPM (stock oil pan + windage)
- BOV leak causing rich/lean transitions under boost
- Idle air control motor (IAC) issues causing stalls or hunting idle
