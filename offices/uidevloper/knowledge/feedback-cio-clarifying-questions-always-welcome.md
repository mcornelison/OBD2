CIO directive 2026-05-22 (case-design session opening): "you are always allowed
to ask clarifying questions at any time during this project or on any other
prompt."

**Why:** Mike steers in real-time via short directives ([[user_mike_collaborative_advisor]]).
Short directives can leave gaps. He'd rather answer a focused question up-front
than have me design from a wrong assumption and discard the work. Asking
respects his time more than guessing does.

**How to apply:**
- When a directive has a load-bearing ambiguity (a dimension, a material, a
  position, a hard constraint), ASK before producing artifacts. One concrete
  question is cheap; a wrong STL is expensive.
- Bundle clarifications — list 2-4 focused questions together rather than
  one-at-a-time ping-pong. Use `AskUserQuestion` when options are
  enumerable; plain text when open-ended.
- This does NOT override the lane-discipline / "minimize y/n" guidance —
  decide-and-proceed on reversible choices (font weight, render preview
  angle, file naming). Ask on irreversible-or-load-bearing choices (case
  outer dimensions, mounting hole pattern, port window geometry, magnet
  count/positions).
- Applies across the whole project, not just case design. Carry forward
  every session.
