**Verdict on bigDoD #1 wording**: PASS. The criterion was "engine crank within boot-grace + 3-min wait + key-off fires sequencer; was silent 5.5 min pre-fix." Sequencer fired ~10s after key-off (V0.27.15 would have been silent 5.5 min per Atlas's F-7 reproduction). Criterion met on the literal reading.

**Caveat I must flag in the report**: the powerwatch journal showed ZERO GPIO6 events during the entire 13-minute engine-running window. The 2-second polling cadence did not catch a GPIO6 LOST transient from the cranking event. Either (a) the battery droop during cranking wasn't deep enough to drop GPIO6 below threshold, (b) it was sub-poll-tick (under 2 seconds), or (c) cranking happened just after boot-grace ended. **This means we may not have empirically generated the specific in-grace + post-grace-LOW conjunction that Atlas's F-7 reproducer required.** The integrated system works correctly in this scenario; whether THIS specific test exercised F-7's exact failure path remains open.

Atlas's code review of the F-7 fix (level-based post-grace check at `__main__.py:32-261`, verified on disk pre-drill) carries the architectural validation separately from the IRL test. The IRL test confirms "no regression on the integrated path." That, plus Atlas's design-gate sign-off, is the practical chain-merge bar — but worth being honest in the test report.
