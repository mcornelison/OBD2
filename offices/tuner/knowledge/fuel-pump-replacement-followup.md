# Fuel Pump Replacement Followup

> Vehicle followup tracked by Spool. Migrated 2026-05-18 from `~/.claude/.../project_fuel_pump_replacement_followup.md` per CIO directive.

When Mike replaces the OEM fuel pump with the planned upgraded pump (likely Walbro 255 or similar), remind him to verify the cold-start symptom is fixed.

**Symptom to verify gone:** First key-on of the day (after car has sat 10+ hours) — engine should START IMMEDIATELY on first crank, no need for 2-3 key cycles to prime the rail.

**Why:** Current OEM pump has a leaking check valve at the pump outlet. Rail pressure bleeds back to tank during long sits. Pre-replacement workaround is multiple key-on cycles to build rail pressure. Upgraded pump's check valve should hold residual pressure overnight → single-crank start.

**How to apply:** Next time Mike mentions the fuel pump install is complete OR mentions the new pump in any context, ask him to do an overnight-sit cold-start test. If symptom persists post-install, suspect alternative leak paths:
- Leaking injector (would also produce fuel smell at engine bay)
- Fuel pressure regulator diaphragm leak (fuel in vacuum line)
- External check valve required (sometimes needed even with new pump)
